<h2>Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students</h2>

<table>
    <tr>
        <th>#</th>
        <th>Safety Tip</th>
        <th>Actionable Steps</th>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>1</td>
        <td>Use Strong Passwords</td>
        <td>Create unique passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password across multiple platforms.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>2</td>
        <td>Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)</td>
        <td>Turn on 2FA for email, banking apps, and social media accounts for an extra layer of security.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>3</td>
        <td>Be Cautious of Links and Messages</td>
        <td>Do not click on unknown links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email. Verify the sender before taking any action.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>4</td>
        <td>Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks</td>
        <td>Do not access banking apps or enter passwords when using public Wi-Fi networks.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>5</td>
        <td>Keep Devices Updated</td>
        <td>Regularly update your phone and laptop to protect against the latest security vulnerabilities.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr class="important">
        <td>6</td>
        <td>Never Share UPI PIN or OTP</td>
        <td>Do not share your UPI PIN, OTP, or banking details with anyone—even if they claim to be from the bank.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr class="important">
        <td>7</td>
        <td>Verify Payment Requests Carefully</td>
        <td>Always check the name and purpose before approving any UPI collect request or scanning a QR code.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>8</td>
        <td>Download Apps from Trusted Sources</td>
        <td>Install apps only from official stores like Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
        <td>9</td>
        <td>Log Out from Shared Devices</td>
        <td>Always log out of your accounts when using college or shared computers.</td>
    </tr>
</table>
